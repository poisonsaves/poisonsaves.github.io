Introduction

Muslims have made Paradise a child’s play. Some Popular beliefs (all of them myths) among Muslims related to paradise are:

  1. Declare that God is One at least once in the life time.
  2. Be part of the saved sect among the 73 sects.
  3. Do one single good deed in your life time.
  4. Follow a Shaikh / Spiritual Leader / Awliya / Alim etc.

If you are a tradition Muslim, the above may not ring any bells in your ears. But if you are an independent and a logical thinker, you are bound to question – really?

Paradise of Quran

The Paradise of Quran is significantly different than the Paradise what Muslims have been believing in.At different places, Quran talks about qualities of people who will enter paradise. Let us ponder over one such instance.

...who are people of understanding (who think, who ponder, who use their intellect) – Quran 13:9

Note from the above ayah that the ability to think, reason, analyze and then believe is the way of Quran. There are many other ayahs, where Quran has asked its followers to reason and believe, to understand and believe.

Those who fulfill the covenant of Allah and do not break the contract - Quran 13:20

Signifying an important quality – of fulfilling the contracts and keeping up the promises – be it to God or their fellow humans.

And those who join that which Allah has ordered to be joined and fear their Lord and are afraid of the evil of [their] account - Quran 13:21

Quran strongly commands unification and strictly prohibits division of people into sects or cutting off relationships. There are numerous ayahs of Quran that have strongly condemned division into sects and in fact some of them have even equated sects with shirk ( associating other gods with the One God).

And those who are patient, seeking the countenance of their Lord, and establish prayer and spend from what We have provided for them secretly and publicly and prevent evil with good - those will have the good consequence of [this] home - Quran 13:22

Many important points are to be noted from the above ayah. Firstly, the meaning of the word “sabr” has been grossly limited to Patience by main stream Muslims scholars. Sabr is an extremely comprehensive virtue that encompasses perseverance, endurance, forbearance, diligence, and restraint. It is simply a characteristic of enlightenment that develops in the heart of a true believer.

Umpteen number of times Quran has asked believers to establish Salaah. It includes purification of an individual, purification of the society and establishing a just social system. Salaah includes far more than 5 times of daily prayers – details of which is beyond the scope of this article.

To summarize, against the popular beliefs and simplification adopted by the Muslims in relation to deserving an entry to Paradise, Quran has provided a list of very comprehensive qualities that believers must possess to qualify an entry to paradise. A lot of these are achieved as a result of significant sacrifice and love towards the Creator. The below were covered earlier in this article.

  1. Rational and Intellectual mind.
  2. Fulfilling of contracts and keeping up of promises.
  3. Fearing God that one will be held accountable for all deeds.
  4. Working for unification and avoiding sects.
  5. Being patient (Sabr).
  6. Establishing Salah.
  7. Spending in the way of God.
  8. Promoting good and preventing evil.
Summary Gardens of perpetual residence; they will enter them with whoever were righteous among their fathers, their spouses and their descendants. And the angels will enter upon them from every gate (saying) Peace be upon you for what you patiently endured. And excellent is the final home." – Quran 13:23-24.
